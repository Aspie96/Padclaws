Nostr

Nostr is a communication protocol for decentralized social networking. Each Client (the software operated by users) uses the protocol to communicate with any number of Relays (which are Nostr servers).

Each Nostr user has a Public Key (a public unique identifier) and the corresponding Private Key (a value used to certify operations by the user).

Nostr provides no way of recovering or deactivating a private key. If you lose your private key, you lose your account. If somebody gets access to your private key, that party gets unfettered access to your account. It is therefore important to store one's key securely.

Information published by a user to Relays (such as text notes) contains the user's Private Key. Deleting data which has been previously published may be impossible.

Relays

The Software is a Nostr Client, which you can use to communicate with any number of Nostr Relays of your choice.

Nostr Relays are controlled independently by third parties. The Admin does not determine the purpose or means of any action (including the processing of personal data) by third-party relays and is not the data controller for any processing of personal data they may perform.

Communication between your device and Relays happens directly. The Service does not act as a third party or a carrier for those communications. Padclaws never communicates with any Relay which you haven't configured without your consent.

You can set the list of used relays in the Relays page. The page may inform you of the existence of some known third-party Relays. This does not indicate any endorsement of those Relays nor any relationship between the Admin and these Relays.

Padclaws stores your Relay configurations, including the list of the relays you use, on your device.
